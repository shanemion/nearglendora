Mantra Coffee Co.

Though perhaps the most "indie" of the three shops presented here having been built inside a home, Mantra delivers the coziest atmosphere. Offering a variety of seating, pastries, and teas and coffees, Mantra feels like home no matter the hour.

Classic Coffee

Located in the heart of Downtown Glendora, Classic is a, well, classic meeting spot for many. Whether it be for business, studying, or just a good time, the central location paired with both the indoor and outdoor seating are hard to beat.

Class One Donuts

A two minute drive away from Glendora High School, Class One Donuts is perfect for students looking for that early boost to their day. Whether it be with their strong, solid coffee or their cheap donut holes, Class One is an easy recommend for locals.

Parks

Finkbiner Park

Hosting a skate park, tennis courts, a playground, a mainstage, as well as ample seating, shade, and picnic grass, Finkbiner is the go to spot for many locals. It's also home to many city-wide events, such as the annual Easter Egg Hunts and Halloween Festivals.

Frank G. Bonelli Regional Park

Just a 10 minute drive down the freeway, Bonelli offers so much. Featuring attractions such as a huge lake for boating, endless trails for hiking or biking, and bountiful group picnic shelters, it's no wonder that Bonelli is so popular. There's always something new to explore!

South Hills Park

A five minute walk away from the Glendora-famous Donut Man, South Hills Park serves as a base camp for a series of trails overlooking the cities of Glendora and San Dimas. Hosting a lively dog park and modest playground, South Hills is a solid option for any family.

Food

Cabo Tacos

Cabo Tacos, formerly Baja Tacos, is home to the best fish tacos in town; only $1.50 on their Fish Taco Wednesday, they can't be beat. Other options include ceviche, classic meat tacos, and fresh horchata. It's a fresh, solid choice to fill you up for cheap!

Legends Classic Diner

Located on the famous Route 66, Legends encapsulates the 50's' experience. With classic car shows every 1st Sunday of the month, surfboards on every wall, and a jukebox, all that's left to do is to grab a milkshake and a pastrami sandwich and enjoy the show.

Miyako Sushi

A personal and local favorite, Miyako sushi is just all around solid. Grab your friends and family during happy hour and go choose from their selection of Japanese essentials with deals on bowls, bento, rolls (their golden tiger roll is amazing!), tempura, and more.

Explore

Glendora Mountain Road

Biking or driving up the 20 mile Glendora Mountain Road amidst a splendid view of Glendora and the foothills is sweet on its own, but it's also home to many offshooting adventures such as the featured Bridge to Nowhere or the hidden Lewis Falls.

Glendora Shopping Center

Nearly anything you need can be found at the Glendora Shopping Center. Explore businesses such as AMC, Best Buy, and Barnes & Noble. Then, take pictures on the roof of the 24 Hour Fitness parking garage before heading down to the nearby Louie Pompei Sports Park.

Hit the Road

Though possessing a modest arsenal itself, one of Glendora's strengths is its central location in sunny Southern California. Get on the highway! Travel 20 minutes to Claremont, 30 to Disneyland, or an hour to the beach. The world is big; please don't spend it all here!
